In this part of the series, we’ll look at some value-centric measures for AT T (T) among the major companies in the US wireless space. Let’s start with the size of the top four US wireless carriers: Verizon ( VZ ), AT T, T-Mobile ( TMUS ), and Sprint (S). As of February 9, 2017, AT T was the largest US telecom player, Verizon was the second largest, and T-Mobile was the third-largest player by market capitalization. Also, Sprint’s market capitalization remained lower than T-Mobile’s. Price-based multiples take into account value from a shareholder’s perspective. EV-based (enterprise value) multiples help investors to understand the value of a company via its sources of capital from the shareholders’ point of view. These are forward multiples based on expected values after a year. Let’s look at the earnings multiples for AT T and its peers as of February 9, 2017. AT T was trading at a forward PE (price-to-earnings) multiple of ~13.59x, which was more than Verizon’s ~12.24x. AT T’s forward EV-to-EBITDA (enterprise value to earnings before interest, tax, depreciation, and amortization) metric was ~6.78x, which was higher than Verizon and Sprint at ~6.62x and ~6.15x, respectively.
