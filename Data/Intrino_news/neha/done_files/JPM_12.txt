<p>TORONTO--(BUSINESS WIRE)-- Demonstrating continued support of Canadian small businesses, <strong>Chase Paymentech</strong> announced today it is extending its partnership with the <strong>Canadian Federation of Independent Business</strong> (CFIB) through April 2022. &ldquo;<strong>Chase Paymentech</strong> has been a terrific partner for Canadian small and medium-sized businesses and the CFIB is happy to renew our partnership with <strong>Chase</strong>, as they serve our members well and offer the lowest payments processing costs available to smaller firms,&rdquo; said Dan Kelly, CEO of the 109,000-member organization. The extension means CFIB members continue to receive preferred pricing for merchant services from <strong>Chase Paymentech</strong>, part of <strong>JPMorgan Chase Co</strong>., named the #1 wholly-owned merchant acquirer in the world by The Nilson Report as of April 2016. &ldquo;Helping small businesses around the world is a priority for us, and we look for partners like the CFIB who share that focus,&rdquo; said Sam Jawad, president of <strong>Chase Paymentech</strong> Canada. &ldquo;The scope of the CFIB allows us to deliver safe and secure payment processing with extra value.&rdquo;<strong> Chase Merchant Services</strong> is the global payment acceptance and merchant acquiring business of <strong>JPMorgan Chase Co</strong>. and processes all major credit and debit card payments as well as mobile payments for clients, while making it a seamless transaction for their customers. In 2016, the firm topped more than $1 trillion in global payments processing. The company employs nearly 400 people in Canada.
