Analyst actions on Thursday included price-target hikes for <strong>Cisco Systems</strong> ( CSCO ), <strong>NetApp</strong> ( NTAP ), <strong>Applied Materials</strong> ( AMAT ) and <strong>CBS</strong> ( CBS ), while <strong>Wells Fargo</strong> ( WFC ) got a downgrade. UBS raised its price target for the networking equipment company to 37 from 35, and Deutsche Bank hiked its target to 40, after <strong>Cisco</strong> topped second-quarter earnings estimates and increased its dividend late Wednesday. Both firms have buy ratings on <strong>Cisco</strong>. UBS analyst Steven Milunovich sees M As in <strong>Cisco's</strong> future, saying even big deals will be considered. While a tax cut on profits held overseas, as planned by the Trump administration, could help facilitate a deal, Milunovich doesn't see a repatriation as being essential for a deal to happen. Shares of <strong>Cisco</strong> closed up 2.4% at 33.60 on the stock market today . The stock is now extended from a 31.99 buy point cleared this week. Analysts hiked their price targets on the maker of storage and data-management products after it topped fiscal Q3 estimates and grew revenue for the first time in 13 quarters. UBS lifted its target on NetApp to 37 from 32, but kept a sell rating. Analyst Steven Milunovich noted lower gross margins, which suggests discounting helped revenue grow. Piper Jaffray lifted its target to 49 from 46, with an overweight rating. Analyst Andrew Nowinski thinks NetApp has "turned the corner" and that revenue can rise by mid-single-digit percentages. Shares advanced 4.2% to finish at 40.56 after rising as high as 42.18 in the morning.
