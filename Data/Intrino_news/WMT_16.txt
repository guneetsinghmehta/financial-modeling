Walmart ( WMT ) is scheduled announce its fiscal 4Q17 and full-year fiscal 2017 results on February 21. The retail giant’s stock was up 12.8% in 2016 and performed better than the S P 500 Index (SPX), which was up 9.5%. However, on a YTD (year-to-date) basis, Walmart’s stock price has fallen 2.0% to $67.77 as of February 13. The S P 500 Index has moved up by 4.0% on a YTD basis as of February 13.   As of February 13, the stock prices of Walmart peers Target ( TGT ), Costco ( COST ), Kroger ( KR ), Dollar Tree ( DLTR ), Dollar General ( DG ), and Whole Foods Market ( WFM ) have given returns of -10.3%, 7.4%, -3.8%, 0.7%, 2.6%, and -2.4%, respectively, since the start of the year. On a YTD basis, Walmart’s stock has underperformed the Consumer Staples Select Sector SPDR Fund ( XLP ), which has risen 3.7%. The XLP ETF has 5.4% exposure to Walmart. In the first nine months of fiscal 2017, which ended on October 31, 2016, Walmart’s revenue rose 0.7% to $354.9 billion. Higher same-stores sales in the first nine months of fiscal 2017 were offset by $8.3 billion of adverse currency movements and a decline of $553 million in fuel sales due to lower fuel prices at the company’s Sam’s Club segment.
