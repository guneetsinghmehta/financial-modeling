Here are some of the stocks the Yahoo Finance team will be watching for you today. PepsiCo ( PEP ) delivered better-than-expected earnings and revenue for the fourth quarter. Revenue rose 5% from a year ago thanks to stronger demand for healthier beverages and snacks in North America. However, the company said the strong dollar hurt results overseas and that challenges in the global economy will continue this year. Apple ( AAPL ) shares are eyeing a fresh high this morning after Warren Buffett’s Berkshire Hathaway unveiled in a regulatory filing that it nearly quadrupled its stake in Apple stock, bringing his total investment to 57.4 million shares last quarter. Buffett, who has been aggressively buying stocks since the election, also increased his stake in the four biggest airlines stocks — Delta Air Lines ( DAL ), American Airlines ( AAL ), Southwest ( LUV ) and United Continental ( UAL ). Buffett was not the only one on a shopping spree in the fourth quarter. Trian Fund Management co-founded by activist investor Nelson Peltz bought a $3.5 billion dollar stake in Procter Gamble ( PG ). The holding represents Trian’s largest position in a company ever, according to Reuters. This comes as P G looks for ways to boost sales and shed unprofitable brands. Fortress Investment ( FIG ) shares surged in early trading. The US-based private equity firm is being bought by Japan’s tech conglomerate Softbank for $3.3 billion in cash. That translates to $8.08 a share, a 39% premium based on Fortress’ closing price on Tuesday. The deal comes after Softbank founder Masayoshi Son announced in October it was teaming up with Saudi Arabia to set up a $100 billion technology fund.
